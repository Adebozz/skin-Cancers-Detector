Image Classifier

Basal cell carcinoma is the most common type of skin cancer. It arises from the basal cells in the lower epidermis, which are responsible for producing new skin cells. Basal cell carcinoma often appears as a waxy bump, a non-healing sore, or a pinkish patch on the skin.

Although basal cell carcinoma rarely spreads to other parts of the body, it can cause local damage if left untreated. Prompt medical evaluation and treatment are recommended if you notice any abnormal skin growths or changes.

Squamous cell carcinoma is a common type of skin cancer that develops from the squamous cells in the outermost layer of the skin. It is usually caused by cumulative exposure to the sun over the years and is often found on sun-exposed areas, such as the face, ears, and hands.

While squamous cell carcinoma is typically not as aggressive as melanoma, early detection and treatment are still important. If you notice any persistent scaly patches, open sores, or growths on your skin, it is advisable to seek medical attention.

Melanoma is a type of skin cancer that develops from melanocytes, the cells that produce melanin (the pigment that gives color to the skin, hair, and eyes). It is the most dangerous form of skin cancer and can spread to other parts of the body.

Early detection and treatment of melanoma are crucial for favorable outcomes. If you suspect any changes in your skin, such as new moles or changes in existing moles, it is important to consult a healthcare professional.
